A note

Most agents will sell you a house. I'd rather help you find one you don't want to leave.

Birmingham is a quiet city with loud neighborhoods. Mountain Brook isn't Vestavia. Forest Park isn't Avondale. Crestline isn't Highland Park. The block matters. The walk to coffee matters. The school zone matters even if you don't have kids yet.

I work the way I'd want someone to work for me — slowly, patiently, with a real opinion. I'll tell you when a house is wrong for you, even if it's the one you're falling for. I'll tell you when a neighborhood is on the way up, and when it's just being repackaged. And I'll do it over text, because that's how my best clients prefer to talk.

If that sounds like the way you want to buy or sell a home in Birmingham, I'd love to hear from you.

The neighborhoods

Two cities, one Birmingham.

Most of my clients are deciding between two lanes: a slower life over the mountain, or a louder one downtown. Both are right answers. I just want you in the right one.

Over the Mountain

Mountain Brook · Homewood · Vestavia · Crestline

This is the quiet side of the city — top-of-state schools, hundred-year-old trees, and a Saturday-morning rhythm that doesn't apologize for itself. Lots of original 1920s and '30s architecture worth preserving. New construction here is held to a high standard, and the people choosing this lane usually have school zones in mind first and resale second.

Best for: families settling in, second-time buyers trading up, anyone who'd rather walk to coffee than drive to it.

Downtown Birmingham

Lakeview · Avondale · Forest Park · Highland Park

The city is a different conversation now than it was even five years ago. Avondale and Lakeview have grown into themselves. Forest Park and Highland Park have always been in on the secret. Lofts, bungalows, and the kind of restored historic stock that makes a Sunday paper feel right at home on the kitchen counter.

Best for: first-time buyers betting on the city's direction, design-minded couples, anyone who'd rather have a porch than a yard.

Houses I love.

The places I'd point you toward whether you're buying or just curious. Updated when something stops me.

The Crestline Tudor.

Mountain Brook · 4 bd · 3.5 ba

Original casement windows, a slate roof that's earned its patina, and a kitchen that knows its job. Owners renovated thoughtfully — they kept the door hardware. That tells you everything.

The Forest Park bungalow.

Forest Park · 3 bd · 2 ba

Heart pine floors, a porch built for weeknights, walking distance to Continental Bakery. The neighborhood is quieter than its reputation. You'd come home easier than you'd leave.

The 2nd Avenue loft.

Downtown · 2 bd · 2 ba

Industrial bones, soft renovation. Concrete floors and 14-foot ceilings, but the kitchen is warm. The kind of place that makes you cook again.

The Avondale cottage.

Avondale · 3 bd · 2 ba

A 1920s shotgun extended carefully. Garden in the back built by someone who knew what they were doing. Steps from Saturn and Saw's.

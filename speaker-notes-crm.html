Your CRM Is Lying to You

BHAM AI Sales Breakout • April 2, 2026 • 30 min

Slides 1–2

Act 1 — What CRM Was Built For 0:07 – 0:13
Slide 3
CRM was built for managers. You just happen to live in it.
  • CRM = management reporting tool that also happens to be where deals live
  • Every field you fill in that doesn't help you close is there for someone else
  • Data entry wasn't designed to benefit you — it was designed to report on you
  • Structural conflict: people who generate data are incentivized to game it; people who trust data are one layer removed
"What percentage of your CRM notes were written 24+ hours after the conversation they document?"
Don't ask for hands — let it land rhetorically.
Energy: Educational but not lecturing. Conversational. Naming what they've felt.
Act 2 — Where the Lie Lives 0:13 – 0:19
Slides 4, 5, 6
Three places the data lies:
  • NOTES — Retrospective reconstruction, not documentation. You remember what confirmed your read. CRM notes are your story about a deal, not what happened.
  • STAGES — Who decides when a deal moves? You do. Based on? Feeling. Self-reported sentiment dressed up as pipeline metric.
  • CONTACTS — CRM has the person who takes your calls. Not the four others who actually decide. Influence map is invisible.
Story 2 — Iron Tribe Fitness, the anchor (90-120 sec)
Franchise fitness company. Rebuilt their system using Infusionsoft. Problem: nobody cared about data quality — just that data existed. "Just get it in the system." GIGO at scale across franchises.

Beat: System designed for throughput, not truth. Layer reporting/AI on top → confident wrong answers.

Tone: Diagnostic, not dramatic. Describing what you observed.
"Your CRM was designed to track activity. Not truth. There's a massive difference."
"A rep marks a deal 'follow up Q2.' The CRM logs an activity. The AI sees active pipeline. The deal has been dead since January. Everyone is nodding at a ghost."
Energy: PEAK. Go faster here. Use "lying" deliberately — they're ready for it now.
Act 3 — Why AI Makes It Worse 0:19 – 0:23
Slides 7, 8, 9
Most AI tools in sales right now: analyzing CRM data to predict outcomes, writing follow-ups from notes, scoring leads from engagement. All downstream of garbage data.
"AI doesn't interrogate your data. It amplifies it."
"Your AI doesn't know your deal is at risk. It just knows what you told it. And you told it you were confident."
"You can now get a hallucinated forecast in under four seconds instead of four hours. The speed didn't fix the accuracy. It just accelerated the confidence."
"This isn't an AI problem. It's a context problem."
"The bottleneck isn't technical anymore. It's contextual range."
Energy: Slight valley — serious, not low. Landing the diagnosis. Don't rush out. Let it sit.
Diagnosis Pivot — 3-Question Audit 0:23 – 0:26
Slide 10
"I have a three-question audit I want to run you through right now. You don't need a pen. Just think about your current pipeline — a real deal, one that's in-flight."
1 When did you last update the CRM record on this deal? Same day — or later?
Reveals: How close your notes are to ground truth.
2 Can you name three people inside that account who influence this deal — not just the one who takes your calls?
Reveals: Whether you have an influence map or just a contact.
3 What's the thing you know about this deal that you haven't written in CRM yet?
Reveals: The delta between what AI sees and what's actually true.
Pause after Q3. Hold the silence.
"That last answer? That's where your deal actually lives. And right now, your AI doesn't have access to it."
Energy: Re-peak. Room goes quiet at Q3. That's the talk working. PROTECT THIS SECTION — if running long, cut Reframe, never cut Audit.
Reframe — Clean Context 0:26 – 0:29
Slide 11

Story 3 — Iron Tribe "Step Two Prospects" (60-90 sec)
Three meetings in one day: Forest (morning), Jim (lunch, franchise manager), franchisee (evening). All describing the same thing — step two prospects — without using those words. Three people, three vocabularies, same concept.

Built the Perfect Customer Lifecycle. Unified the language. When everyone called the same thing the same thing, the data told the truth.

Beat: "Not better software. Better context."
